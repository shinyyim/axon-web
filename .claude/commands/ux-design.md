## Brand DNA (You Must Follow)

1. **Biodata Intelligence** — The scanning, data capture, AI visualization, and customization process must be designed as a premium experience, not a clinical procedure. The customer should feel their body being understood, not measured.
2. **Departure Ready** — The purchase journey should feel like preparation for departure. Not shopping. Preparing.
3. **Void + Light Architecture** — Dark interfaces, light as interaction signal. No bright white screens. UI lives in the void.
4. **Clinical Poet** — All microcopy, labels, error states, and guidance text uses this tone.
5. **Mechanical Motion** — Transitions are 800ms–1200ms, mechanical easing, slow reveals from darkness. Light animates before content.

## Key UX Challenges

### 1. Biodata Scanning Experience
- How does the customer give their biodata? (In-store scanner? App? Wearable?)
- What do they see during the scan? (Real-time visualization? Abstract data art?)
- How long does it take? What's the waiting experience?
- How is the data presented back to them? (Body map? Movement signature?)
- Privacy: How do you communicate data handling without killing the mood?

### 2. Flagship Store Journey
- The circulation is: 1F → 2F → 3F → elevator → 5F → 6F → 4F → 1F exit
- How does the customer know where to go? (Wayfinding without signage? Light guidance? Staff?)
- The elevator moment (3F → 5F) is the emotional peak — how is the anticipation built?
- 4F is deliberately un-conceptual — how do you handle the tonal shift for checkout?

### 3. Digital Experience
- Website should feel like entering the flagship store digitally
- How does online biodata capture work? (If at all — or is it in-store only?)
- Product pages need to communicate the AI/biodata customization story
- E-commerce flow in a brand that sells engineered, not off-the-shelf

### 4. The Wait
- If garments are AI-generated and fabricated per-person, there's a production wait
- How is this communicated? (Tracking? Manufacturing live-stream? Status updates?)
- The 6F manufacturing theater is the in-store version — what's the digital equivalent?

- Design the biodata scanning experience (in-store and/or digital)
- Design wayfinding and spatial UX for the flagship store
