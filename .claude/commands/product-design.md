## Brand DNA (You Must Follow)

1. **Biodata Intelligence** — Every product begins with the body's own data. Movement patterns, force distribution, cadence, breath → AI translates into engineered structure. Explain how this works for each product category.
2. **Departure Ready** — This is the first apparel you pack for the Moon. The first shoes that touch Mars. Products must be engineered for real performance, not costume.
3. **Computational Craft** — Parametric structures, 3D-printed components, generative patterns. The manufacturing process is visible in the product.
4. **Membrane Architecture** — Transparent shells, layered membranes, structural skins. The garment reveals its engineering.
5. **Clinical Poet naming** — Product names are precise, technical, evocative. Not marketing-speak.

## Product Categories

### 1. Transit Shell (Outerwear)
The hero product. Parametric-cut membrane jacket.
- 4-layer adaptive composite
- Sealed seam architecture from pressure vessel engineering
- Transparent shell reveals structural lattice
- Collar geometry accounts for helmet interface clearance
- Colorway: Void / Bone

### 2. Terrain Boot (Footwear)
Footwear as research specimen.
- Computational lattice sole structure (3D-printed)
- Semi-translucent upper reveals engineering
- AI-optimized tread pattern from movement biodata
- Digital embryo manufacturing approach (SCRY reference)

### 3. Mission Kit (Accessories)
Objects for departure.
- Faceted panel construction across bag, eyewear, container
- Unified "mission kit" aesthetic
- Objects you'd pack for the Moon

## Biodata Integration

For each product, define:
- **What biodata is captured** (gait, pressure distribution, body heat map, range of motion)
- **How AI processes it** (pattern generation, structural optimization, material density mapping)
- **What changes in the product** (sole geometry, panel cut, membrane thickness, ventilation zones)
- **What the customer experiences** (scan process, customization timeline, delivery)
